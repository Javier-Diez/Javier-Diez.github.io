Streamlined Study Plan

Year 1: Core Foundations

  • Fall Semester:
    14.01: Principles of Microeconomics (12 credits)
    15.501: Introduction to Financial and Managerial Accounting (12 credits)
  • Total: 24 credits (side courses)
  • Spring Semester:
    15.401: Finance Theory I (9 credits)
  • Total: 9 credits (side courses)

Year 2: Building Finance Expertise

  • Fall Semester:
    14.30: Introduction to Statistical Methods in Economics (12 credits) – builds statistical and analytical skills.
  • Total: 12 credits (side courses)
  • Spring Semester:
    15.402: Finance Theory II (9 credits) – builds on 15.401.
    15.010: Economic Analysis for Business Decisions (9 credits).
    15.279: Management Communication for Undergraduates (9 credits) – enhances communication skills valuable for finance and engineering.
  • Total Credits for the Semester: 27 credits

Year 3: Advanced Topics

  • Fall Semester:
    15.430: Entrepreneurial Finance (9 credits) – requires 15.401.
    Managerial Psychology Laboratory (9 credits, optional but recommended for CI-M).
  • Total: 18 credits (side courses)
  • Spring Semester:
    15.433: Financial Markets (9 credits) – builds on 15.401.
    15.450: Analytics of Finance (12 credits).
    15.414: Financial Management (9 credits) – Corporate finance and capital markets, including asset valuation, risk management, and corporate financing. – builds on 15.401.
  • Total: 30 credits (side courses)

Year 4: Specialized Electives

  • Fall Semester:
    15.518: Taxes and Business Strategy (9 credits) – requires 15.501.
    15.535: Business Analysis Using Financial Statements (9 credits) – requires 15.501 and advanced finance coursework.
    15.511: Financial Accounting (9 credits) – Focus on corporate financial accounting and reporting, with an emphasis on managerial decision-making and investment analysis.
  • Total: 27 credits (side courses)
  • Spring Semester:
    15.483: Consumer Finance: Markets, Product Design, and FinTech (9 credits).
    15.S08: FinTech: Shaping the Financial World (9 credits, elective).
    15.761: Introduction to Operations Management (9 credits) – valuable for understanding operational strategies in finance and engineering.
  • Total Credits for the Semester: 27 credits

Final Notes

  • Total Credits for Side Courses: Approximately 138 credits across 4 years (manageable alongside engineering).
  • Key Milestones:
    By Year 2, you’ll have completed all foundational finance courses.
    By Year 3, you’ll be eligible for advanced topics and electives.

Flexibility:

  • If workload gets heavy, you can delay electives like 15.483 or 15.S08 to the final semester.
  • Some courses like 15.310 (Managerial Psychology Laboratory) are optional if you don’t need CI-M.

This plan ensures you steadily build expertise in finance and economics while keeping the workload balanced with your primary engineering studies.
